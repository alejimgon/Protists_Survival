Eukaryotic tree of live main groups:
Here, I will add all the relevant information about the diferent supergroups and and different subgroups (if relevant)

- TSAR:
    - Telonemia: Heterotrophic free-living flagellate
    - SAR
        - Stramenopila: 
            - Photosynthesic: Derived ultimately from a red algae.
                - Diatoms (Bacillariophyta): Unicellular/colonial with bipartite siliceous "cell walls"
                - Brown algae (Phaeophyta)
            - Heterotrophic:
                - Fungus-like: Generally parasite plants or aquatic organims
                - Flagellated: One or two flagella
                - Opalinata: two to four flagella, multiflagellated and non flagellated anaerobic Blastocystis
        - Alveolata:
            - Apicomplexa: Non-phosynthetic plastids. Parasitic (Plasmodium, Toxoplasma gondii, Cryptosporidium parvum)
            - Colpodellids: predate upon other protist or parasite them
            - Chromerids: phosynthetic
            - Dinoflagellata: autotrophs and/or grazers (many are mixotrophs)
            - Ciliophora (ciliates): (mostly) free-living heterotrophic. Most have large number of cillia. Nuclear dimorphism (somatic macronuclei and germline micronuclei)
        - Rhizaria: heterotrophic amoebae, flagellates and amoeboflagellates, spore-forming parasites, unusual algae
            - Foraminifera: 
            - Radiolarian
- Haptista:
    - Centrohelida: free-living protozoa with ray-like pseudopodia supported by microtubules (axopodia), which radiate from a spherical cell body.
    - Haptophyta: Plastid of red-algae origin 
    - Rappermonads: Unicellular algae
- Cryptista
    - Palpitomonas: Heterotrophic biflagellate
    - Katablepharida: Heterotrophic biflagellate
    - Cryptophyta: Two flagella and plastid with red algae origin
- Archaeplastida:
    - Rhodophyta: red algae
    - Rhodelphis: heterotrophic flagellates with a nonphotosynthetic primary plastid
    - Chloroplastida: land plants and green algae
    - Glaucophyta: 
- Ancoracysta: Heterotrophic free-living flagellate
- Picozoa: Heterotrophic free-living flagellate
- Amorphea:
    - Obazoa
        - Apusomonada: Heterotrophic flagellate

        - Opisthokonta: Animals, fungi, and their respective unicellular relatives
    - Amoebozoa: free-living (and parasites???) amoeboid forms with lobose pseudopodia (e.g., Amoeba)but also more filose amoebae, some flagellates, and various slime molds.
